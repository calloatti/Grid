1. BlockOccupations (The "Voxel Blueprint")
This enum defines exactly which "parts" of a single tile a building claims. It is a bitmask, meaning a single building can occupy multiple parts at once.

None (0): Used for "skin" objects (like your Impermeable Wall) that exist visually but don't stop movement or other builds.

All (-1): Fills the entire block. Nothing else can be built here.

Floor (1) & Bottom (2): Usually claimed by paths, platforms, or the base of buildings.

Middle (32) & Top (4): Claimed by walls, tall buildings, or the upper half of a voxel.


Path (16): A specific flag used for roads to prevent overlapping with other path-based logic.

2. MatterBelow (The Foundation Rules)
The MatterBelowValidator uses this enum to check if a building is "floating" or stable.

Ground: Must be placed directly on the terrain or a block that acts like terrain.

Stackable: Must have a building below it that is marked as BlockStackable (like a platform or a solid building).

Air: The block actually requires "empty" space below it to be valid.

Any: The building can be placed anywhere (floating, on land, or on water). This is what you used for your Impermeable Wall to allow side-attachment.

GroundOrStackable: The most common setting; it accepts either natural terrain or beaver-made platforms.

3. BlockStackable (Building "Up")
This determines if the top surface of a building can support more buildings.

None: No stacking allowed.

BlockObject: Allows other buildings to be placed on top of this one (e.g., placing a house on a platform).

UnfinishedGround: Used for specific terraforming or landscaping objects.

4. BlockObjectLayout (The Mouse Tools)
This enum controls how the build-tool behaves when you click and drag in the game.

Single: One click, one building.

Rectangle: Drags out a 2D box of buildings (e.g., planting trees or placing dirt blocks).

Line: Drags out a single straight line (e.g., paths, levees, or dams).

SideLine: Specifically used for objects that align along the edges of tiles (e.g., floodgates or sluices).

5. The BlockValidator (The "Judge")
This service runs every frame while you are placing a building to determine if the "Preview" is green (valid) or red (invalid). It checks:

FitsInMap: Ensures you aren't building outside the map or too high.

ConflictsWithExistingObject: Checks the BlockOccupations bitmask of the new building against the WorldBlock data already in that tile.

ConflictsWithTerrain: Ensures a non-underground building isn't inside a cliff.

MatterBelow: Calls the MatterBelowValidator to ensure the foundation is correct.

6. BlockObjectState (The Lifecycle)
Every building on the map is in one of three states:

Preview: The "ghost" building following your mouse. It is not yet part of the BlockService and does not block water.

Unfinished: A construction site. Beavers can walk through it, but it claims the space so no other buildings can be placed there.

Finished: The final building. It now activates all its "Specs" (like water blocking, power generation, or housing).
